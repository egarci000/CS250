### SNHU CS250 Sprint Review and Retrospective README





During the SNHU Travel project, I interpreted user needs and implemented them into the program by understanding their problems from their perspective, in their own language. Users needed several features in an ideal Travel app, they wanted to be able to customize their profile and add details relevant to their vacation needs and wants. They wanted the option to filter the vacation offers the app presented to them. Knowing these needs relied heavily on the user stories that were presented by the Product Owner. They helped translate their broad needs into tasks that the development team could accomplish. By breaking down the stories into clear acceptance criteria and test cases the team was able to build the features the user wanted in a clean, efficient way.



I approach developing programs by first identifying what need I'm trying to incorporate into the program. Then by creating a diagram and pseudocode to give me a greater sense of what I will be developing. Then I write the code and test throughout. There are several Agile processes I hope to incorporate in my future development work. I want to create clearly define acceptance criteria, development backlogs, and test cases. I also hope that any company I work for will incorporate practices such as Sprint Planning, and Daily Scrums. These Agile practices encourage better communication, team synergy, and help deliver great projects.



To be a good team member in software development is to be able to communicate clearly, knowing your roles and responsibilities within the team, and being able to deliver work to support the team objective. In an Agile framework there are several roles that one can have, whether it be Product Owner, Scrum Master, tester, or developer. Being able to excel in whichever one of those roles you find yourself in, means being a good team member, committed to the development and team objectives.
